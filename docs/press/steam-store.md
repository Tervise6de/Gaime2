## 2. About This Game (full description — paste into the rich-text editor)

**Petty Kingdoms is a compact 4X strategy game about small realms with big
grudges — and the people who rule them.** No sprawling all-nighter: a full game
is a satisfying evening. You explore a procedurally generated realm, build an
economy, manage a restless population, raise armies under named commanders, and
out-manoeuvre rival lords for the crown — while the game quietly writes the
chronicle of your reign.

**The map has faces, and the run tells a story.**

- **Named rivals, not colours.** Every realm is led by a generated ruler with a
  reputation — *Visvaldis the Cruel*, *Konrad the Shrewd* — and diplomacy speaks
  in their name. You're not fighting "the red blob"; you're fighting a person.
- **Commanders who matter — and who can betray you.** Put a general at the head
  of an army and their skill sharpens its attack and defence. But a disloyal
  commander in a province you've let fester will **turn his coat** — seizing your
  own land and leading the revolt against you as a named pretender.
- **A chronicle of every reign.** Wars, revolts, betrayals, the fall of a rival,
  the final victory — each is recorded in prose as it happens, building a
  run-long saga you can read back and screenshot.

**Depth comes from interacting systems, not spectacle.** Every number on screen
is a lever you can reason about:

- **Economy & population** — terrain feeds a production web of gold, food,
  materials and knowledge. Grow your regions, but watch food (famine bites) and
  gold (bankruptcy disbands your troops).
- **Unrest, the anti-snowball brake** — high taxes, over-expansion and fresh
  conquests raise unrest; a province left to seethe secedes under a rebel
  pretender. Runaway empires pay for their reach.
- **Army stacking & combined battles** — split, merge and reinforce stacks;
  **fortify** to dig in over turns; an enemy army's **zone of control** pins the
  ground around it; and when you strike a defended province, its neighbours — and
  its **allies** — rally to one combined defence.
- **A nine-unit counter loop** — militia, infantry, ranged, cavalry, siege,
  pikemen, handgunners, swordsmen and knights form a rock-paper-scissors of
  composition. "Spam the strongest unit" never wins; siege alone cracks walls.
- **Diplomacy with teeth** — war, peace, pacts, alliances, gifts, tribute and
  trade. Rivals have personalities and read your weakness; an alliance answers
  the call and drags its ally into your war.
- **A branching 30-tech tree & four paths to victory** — dominate the map,
  complete your Great Works, carry the faith across the world, or lead on
  prestige when the clock runs out.
- **Bounded random events** — harvests, plagues, festivals, uprisings and hard
  choices that reward a resilient realm.

**Play it anywhere, keep it forever.**

- **Runs on your machine, offline.** The rival AI is plain local code — no
  server, no account, no LLM, no key. Nothing phones home.
- **Seeded & shareable.** The same seed produces the same world, the same
  rivals, and the same rulers. Screenshot a brutal opening and challenge a
  friend to the exact war.
- **Fast & readable.** Loads instantly, plays with mouse or keyboard, with a
  colour-blind-safe palette and reduce-motion options built in.

Rule well, or rule what's left.

## 8. Making the desktop (Steam) build

The game is a self-contained static web app, so wrap it:

- **Tauri (recommended)** — Rust webview, ~5 MB binaries, keeps the "tiny"
  ethos; point it at the `dist/` build. Cross-compiles Win/mac/Linux.
- **Electron** — simpler, ~100 MB; fine if you don't want a Rust toolchain.

Map the existing systems onto Steam features (cheap wins): the **achievements
system → Steam achievements**, **save/load → Steam Cloud**, **shareable seeds →
a light community hook**. Add fullscreen/resolution options for the desktop
build.
